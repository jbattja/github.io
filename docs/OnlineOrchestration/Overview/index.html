Welcome to Open Fabric's Developer Portal

Overview​

Companies operating closed-loop consumer-facing payment services (hereafter referred to as Tenant) like wallets, BNPL, etc (hereafter referred to as Payment Method) face a number of challenges when scaling their points of acceptance with Merchants and Payment Gateways (hereafter referred to as Partner).

Integrating with new Payment Methods is difficult for Partners due to the one-time custom integration that is involved, as well as the changes needed to the Partner's back-end for settlement and reconciliation. It usually takes 3-4 months of effort to integrte with a new Payment Method.

The challenges with one to one custom integrations and multiple changes to Partners' financial systems is a major blocker for Tenants when it comes to expanding their Partner (Merchant/Payment Gateway) footprint.

With Open Fabric, Tenants can more easily scale the reach of their Payment Methods through our simple integration stack. Partners that integrate through Open Fabric's SDK/APIs can expect to finish the integration in no more than 3-4 days!

How it works​

When a Tenant integrates to our platform, we are able to orchestrate transactions between their Payment Method and their Partners (Merchants and Payment Gateways). We do this via the use of a virtual credit card token that we hand over to the Partner once a transaction is approved by the Tenant. Partners (especially large merchants and payment gateways) are already familiar with card payments and hence able to use their existing card payment infrastructure to process this virtual card token.

Our platform today supports virtual credit card as the open-loop payment token that we share with Partners, with plans to support other payment token forms in the future.

A Partner with simple (copy-paste style) integration changes can now initiate a transaction with the Payment Method and get a credit card token they can charge through their existing card processing setup.

Settlement of funds will happen through their existing card processing setup as they charged the one-time use virtual card via their card process setup. Partners can facilitate refunds by initiating a refund against this one-time-use virtual card using their card processing setup.

Key Benefits​

  • Simple integration to accept an Open Fabric powered 'Payment Method.'
  • No need for a custom settlement/reconciliation flow. No heavy backend change is needed.
  • Less maintenance and highly scalable way to accept new alternate 'Payment Methods.'
