Tenant Integration Guide

1. Background​

OpenFabric accelerates the adoption of a Payment Method(BNPLs, Wallets, etc.) offered by our Tenants with their Partners (Merchants, Payment Gateways, etc.) by simplifying the technical integration needed between them.

With Open Fabric, Tenants can drastically simplify the integration process with their Partners (Merchants and Payment Gateways) and shorten the integration timeline from months to days.

For each transaction approved by our Tenants, OpenFabric generates and hands over a one-time virtual card to the Partner. The Partner can reuse their existing card processing setup to charge this virtual card. Settlement of funds for the transaction will happen through their card processing setup.

This document provides an overview of all interactions/integration between our Tenants and OpenFabric to enable simplified acceptance of their Payment Method. .

Our Partner Integration page gives you an overview of how Partners integrate today, how our solution makes it better for them accept our Tenant's Payment Methods and integration patterns that we offer to them.

4. Integration Steps - Deep Dive​

Step 1: Expose TenantTransactionRequest API (OpenFabric -> Tenant)​

When your customer would like to pay using your Payment Method on a Partner's payment page (Merchant or Payment Gateway), OpenFabric will place a TenantTransactionRequest to your system.

We expect our Tenants to provide a redirect URL to which we will send the customer for payment approval.

Step 2: Update Transaction Approval Status (Tenant -> OpenFabric)​

Once the customer is redirected to the redirect URL from the above step, the customer will go through Tenant's Payment Approval Flow.

When the Tenant is ready to approve the transaction, OpenFabric expects the approval decision to be updated via our our UpdateTransaction API.

We expect one of the below possible results as an update.

ResultDescription
ApprovedPayment is Approved, and OpenFabric can now hand over a one-time use payment credential to the Partner for this transaction.
FailedPayment is Cancelled/Failed, and OpenFabric should not hand over payment credentials to Partner for this transaction.

In the case of a customer canceling the transaction or if the payment is declined for any other error reasons, update to UpdateTransaction API should include reason.

Refer to our UpdateTransaction API and Schema via the link below.

Update Transaction API and Schema

Step 3: Redirect Customer to Partner​

Once UpdateTransaction API is completed, Tenants should redirect the customer to one of the below OpenFabric Gateway URLs that we provided to you in the TenantTransactionRequest, depending on the result of approval for the transaction.

ResultDescription
ApprovedThe customer is redirected to gateway_success_url.
FailedThe customer is redirected to gateway_fail_url.

OpenFabric will automatically redirect the customer then to the Partner's payment page depending on the transaction result.

Step 4: Consume Card Payment Updates (OpenFabric -> Tenant)​

Post successful approval of a transaction, OpenFabric will hand over a payment token (one-time use virtual credit card) to the Partner. The Partner would process/charge this virtual card using their existing card processing setup.

Whenever the partner charges the virtual card, OpenFabric will send a real-time update about the successful card charge activity to our Tenants.

These notifications will be sent to the webhook URL (as a POST method). The webhook notification will have the authentication header we would have configured for you (as part of the webhook URL setup).

Tenants are requested to consume these notifications and initiate any necessary updates to your workflows (updating status of transactions, etc.) accordingly. For example, you may update your customer's balance once the card charge is successfully completed.

OpenFabric expect a 200 OK response from the webhook endpoint when you successfully receive the weekhook notification. If we do not receive a 200 OK response, Open Fabric will retry for a specific duration. Refer to the Getting Started page to understand how we retry.

Card Charge Update Notification Schema

Step 5: Fetch Transaction Status - optional​

Tenants can also use the Transaction Details API to pull the latest status of a payment transaction. We recommend you call this API for transactions every 20 minutes after approval.
